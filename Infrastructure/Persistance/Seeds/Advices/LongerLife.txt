<h1 class="css-6jxmuv">Habits to Form Now for a Longer Life</h1>
<div>
<p class="css-1rnzyga">Eating a nutritious diet and exercising regularly may increase your life expectancy. Other factors, like overeating and drinking more than a moderate level of alcohol, may increase your risk of certain diseases.</p>
</div>
<p>Many people think that life expectancy is largely determined by genetics.</p>
<p>However, genes play a much smaller role than originally believed. It turns out that environmental factors like&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://healthline.com/nutrition/27-health-and-nutrition-tips">diet and lifestyle are key</a>.</p>
<p>Here are 13 habits linked to a long life.</p>
<h2>1. Avoid overeating</h2>
<p>The link between&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/how-many-calories-per-day">calorie intake</a>&nbsp;and longevity currently generates a lot of interest.</p>
<p>Animal studies suggest that a 10&ndash;50% reduction in normal calorie intake may increase maximum lifespan (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20395504" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Studies of human populations renowned for longevity also observe links between low calorie intake, an extended lifespan, and a lower likelihood of disease (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17986602" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32559388/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" data-type="link" data-id="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32559388/">3<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24291541" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">4<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>What&rsquo;s more, calorie restriction may help reduce excess body weight and&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/6-proven-ways-to-lose-belly-fat">belly fat</a>, both of which are associated with shorter lifespans (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22281163" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">5<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16960025" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">6<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23764746" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">7<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>That said, long-term calorie restriction is often unsustainable and can include negative side effects, such as increased hunger, low body temperature, and a diminished sex drive (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32559388/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" data-type="link" data-id="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32559388/">3<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Whether calorie restriction slows aging or extends your lifespan is not yet fully understood.</p>
<h2>2. Eat more nuts</h2>
<p>Nuts are nutritional powerhouses.</p>
<p>They&rsquo;re rich in protein, fiber, antioxidants, and beneficial plant compounds. What&rsquo;s more, they&rsquo;re a great source of several vitamins and minerals, such as copper,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/10-proven-magnesium-benefits">magnesium</a>, potassium, folate, niacin, and vitamins B6 and E (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17125529" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">8<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Several studies show that nuts have beneficial effects on heart disease, high blood pressure, inflammation, diabetes, metabolic syndrome, belly fat levels, and even some forms of cancer (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3257681/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">9<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24847854" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">10<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20458092" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17125535" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>One study found that people who consumed at least 3 servings of nuts per week had a 39% lower risk of premature death (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23866098" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">13<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Similarly, two recent reviews including over 350,000 people noted that one serving of&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/9-healthy-nuts">nuts</a>&nbsp;per week resulted in 4% and one serving of nuts per day resulted in 27% decreased risk of all-cause mortality and decreased risk of CVD mortality (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4121755/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">14<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/25833976" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">15<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<h2>3. Try out turmeric</h2>
<p>When it comes to anti-aging strategies,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/top-10-evidence-based-health-benefits-of-turmeric">turmeric</a>&nbsp;is a great option. That&rsquo;s because this spice contains a potent bioactive compound called curcumin.</p>
<p>Due to its antioxidant and&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/anti-inflammatory-diet-101">anti-inflammatory</a>&nbsp;properties, curcumin is thought to help maintain brain, heart, and lung function, as well as protect against cancers and age-related diseases (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19594223" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">16<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12676044" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">17<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17569207" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">18<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15650394" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">19<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1357272508002550" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">20</a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20205886" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">21<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26007179" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">22<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Curcumin is linked to an increased lifespan in both insects and mice (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20645870" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">23<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17516143" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">24<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22653297" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">25<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>In vivo and in vitro studies in humans have confirmed that curcumin may help to prevent cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, inflammatory diseases, neurodegenerative disorders, and other conditions (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9881416/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" data-type="link" data-id="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9881416/">26<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Nevertheless, turmeric has been consumed for thousands of years in India and is generally considered safe.</p>
<h2>4. Eat plenty of healthy plant foods</h2>
<p>Consuming a wide variety of plant foods, such as fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, whole grains, and&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/beans-101">beans</a>, may decrease disease risk and promote longevity.</p>
<p>For example, many studies link a plant-rich diet to a lower risk of premature death, as well as a reduced risk of cancer, metabolic syndrome, heart disease, depression, and brain deterioration (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15383513" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">27<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21736834" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">28<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23720230" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">29<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19656644" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">30<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>These effects are attributed to plant foods&rsquo; nutrients and antioxidants, which include polyphenols, carotenoids, folate, and vitamin C (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24210636" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">31<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Accordingly, several studies link vegetarian and vegan diets, which are naturally higher in plant foods, to a 12&ndash;15% lower risk of premature death (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23836264" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://www.karger.com/Article/Abstract/337301" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">33</a>).</p>
<p>The same studies also report a 29&ndash;52% lower risk of dying from cancer or heart, kidney, or hormone-related diseases (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23836264" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://www.karger.com/Article/Abstract/337301" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">33</a>).</p>
<p>What&rsquo;s more, some research suggests that the risk of premature death and certain diseases increases with greater meat consumption (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19307518" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">34<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22412075" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">35<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17435450" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">36<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>However, other studies report either nonexistent or much weaker links &mdash; with the negative effects seeming specifically linked to&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/why-processed-meat-is-bad">processed meat</a>&nbsp;(<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20479151" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">37<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23497300" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">38<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Vegetarians and vegans also generally tend to be more health-conscious than meat eaters, which could at least partly explain these findings.</p>
<p>Overall, eating plenty of plant foods is likely to benefit health and longevity.</p>
<h2>5. Stay physically active</h2>
<p>It should come as no surprise that staying physically active can keep you healthy and add years to your life.</p>
<p>As few as 15 minutes of&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/10-benefits-of-exercise">exercise</a>&nbsp;per day may help you achieve benefits, which could include an additional 3 years of life (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(11)60749-6/abstract" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">39<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Furthermore, your risk of premature death may decrease by 4% for each additional 15 minutes of daily physical activity (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(11)60749-6/abstract" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">39<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>A recent review observed a 22% lower risk of early death in individuals over 60 who exercised &mdash; even though they worked out less than the recommended 150 minutes per week (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26238869" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">40<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>People who hit the 150-minute recommendation were 28% less likely to die early. What&rsquo;s more, that number was 35% for those who exercised beyond this guidance (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/26238869" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">40<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Finally, some research links vigorous activity to a 5% greater reduction in risk compared to low- or moderate-intensity activities (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22039197" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">41<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<h2>6. Don&rsquo;t smoke</h2>
<p>Smoking is strongly linked to disease and early death (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22688992" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">42<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Overall, people who smoke may lose up to 10 years of life and be 3 times more likely to die prematurely than those who never pick up a cigarette (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3547248/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">43<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>A recent review states that quitting tobacco before age 40 will prevent almost all increased risks of death from smoking. Keep in mind that it&rsquo;s never too late to quit (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7093109/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" data-type="link" data-id="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7093109/">44<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>)</p>
<p>One study reports that individuals who quit smoking by age 35 may prolong their lives by up to 8.5 years (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1447499/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">45<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Furthermore, quitting smoking in your 60s may add up to 3.7 years to your life. In fact, quitting in your 80s may still provide benefits (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22688992" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">42<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1447499/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">45<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<h2>7. Moderate your alcohol intake</h2>
<p>Heavy&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/alcohol-good-or-bad">alcohol consumption</a>&nbsp;is linked to liver, heart, and pancreatic disease, as well as an overall increased risk of early death (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22336255" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">46<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>However, moderate consumption is associated with a reduced likelihood of several diseases, as well as a 17&ndash;18% decrease in your risk of premature death (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22336255" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">46<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17159008" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">47<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9824172/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" data-type="link" data-id="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9824172/">48<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p><a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/red-wine-good-or-bad">Wine</a>&nbsp;is considered particularly beneficial due to its high content of polyphenol antioxidants.</p>
<p>Results from a 29-year study showed that men who preferred wine were 34% less likely to die early than those who preferred beer or spirits (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17339649" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">49<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>In addition, one review observed wine to be especially protective against heart disease, diabetes, neurological disorders, and metabolic syndrome (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3407993/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">50<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>To keep consumption moderate, it is recommended that women aim for 1&ndash;2 units or less per day and a maximum of 7 per week. Men should keep their daily intake to less than 3 units, with a maximum of 14 per week (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://health.gov/dietaryguidelines/2015/guidelines/appendix-9/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">51<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>It&rsquo;s important to note that no strong research indicates that the benefits of moderate drinking are greater than those of abstaining from alcohol.</p>
<p>In other words, there is no need to start drinking if you don&rsquo;t usually consume alcohol.</p>
<h2>8. Prioritize your happiness</h2>
<p><a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/happiness-and-health">Feeling happy</a>&nbsp;can significantly increase your longevity (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2199189" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">52</a>).</p>
<p>In fact, happier individuals had a 3.7% reduction in early death over a 5-year study period (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22042845" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">53<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>A study of 180 Catholic nuns analyzed their self-reported levels of happiness when they first entered the monastery and later compared these levels to their longevity.</p>
<p>Those who felt happiest at 22 years of age were 2.5 times more likely to still be alive six decades later (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11374751" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">54<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Finally, a review of 35 studies showed that happy people may live up to 18% longer than their less happy counterparts (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18725425" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">55<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<h2>9. Avoid chronic stress and anxiety</h2>
<p>Anxiety and stress may significantly decrease your lifespan.</p>
<p>For instance, women suffering from stress or anxiety are reportedly up to two times more likely to die from heart disease, stroke, or lung cancer (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19013760" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">56<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2939149/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">57<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12208798" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">58<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Similarly, the risk of premature death is up to three times higher for anxious or stressed men compared to their more relaxed counterparts (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://bjp.rcpsych.org/content/185/5/399.full" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">59</a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15140535" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">60<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18611955" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">61<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>If you&rsquo;re&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/16-ways-relieve-stress-anxiety">feeling stressed</a>, laughter and optimism could be two key components of the solution.</p>
<p>Studies show that pessimistic individuals have a 42% higher risk of early death than more optimistic people. However, both laughter and a positive outlook on life can reduce stress, potentially prolonging your life (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12652882" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">62<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/2556917" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">63<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17165632" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">64<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0960982211011912" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">65</a>).</p>
<h2>10. Nurture your social circle</h2>
<p>Researchers report that maintaining healthy social networks can help you improve your odds of survival by 50% (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6125010/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" data-type="link" data-id="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6125010/">66<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>In fact, having just 3 social ties may decrease your risk of early death by more than 200% (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/11292274" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">67<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Studies also link healthy social networks to positive changes in heart, brain, hormonal, and immune function, which may decrease your risk of chronic diseases (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16758315" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">68<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3150158/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">69<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2729718/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">70<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15760298" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">71<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19711810" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">72<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>A strong social circle might also help you react less negatively to stress, perhaps further explaining the positive effect on lifespan (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7560850" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">73<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10204977" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">74<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Finally, one study reports that providing support to others may be more beneficial than receiving it. In addition to accepting care from your friends and family, make sure to return the favor (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12807404" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">75<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<h2>11. Be more conscientious</h2>
<p>Conscientiousness refers to a person&rsquo;s ability to be self-disciplined, organized, efficient, and goal-oriented.</p>
<p>Based on data from a study that followed 1,500 boys and girls into old age, kids who were considered persistent, organized, and disciplined lived 11% longer than their less conscientious counterparts (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18823176" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">76<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3587967/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">77<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Conscientious people may also have lower blood pressure and fewer psychiatric conditions, as well as a lower risk of diabetes and heart or joint problems (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16908463" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">78<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>This might be partly because conscientious individuals are less likely to take dangerous risks or react negatively to stress &mdash; and more likely to lead successful professional lives or be responsible about their health (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15535742" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">79<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19572784" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">80<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12160-009-9095-6/fulltext.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">81</a>).</p>
<p>Conscientiousness can be developed at any stage in life through steps as small as tidying up a desk, sticking to a work plan, or being on time.</p>
<h2>12. Drink coffee or tea</h2>
<p>Both coffee and tea are linked to a decreased risk of chronic disease.</p>
<p>For instance, the polyphenols and catechins found in&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/top-10-evidence-based-health-benefits-of-green-tea">green tea</a>&nbsp;may decrease your risk of cancer, diabetes, and heart disease (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16311246" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">82<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://aje.oxfordjournals.org/content/167/1/71.abstract" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">83</a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16618952" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">84<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20008687" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">85<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18641205" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">86<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Similarly, coffee is linked to a lower risk of type 2 diabetes, heart disease, and certain cancers and brain ailments, such as Alzheimer&rsquo;s and Parkinson&rsquo;s (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007%2Fs00125-009-1516-3" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">87</a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0167527308008498" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">88</a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22695871" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">89<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/ijc.20989/full" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">90<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20182026" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">91<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/mds.21706/abstract" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">92<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Additionally, both coffee and tea drinkers benefit from a 20&ndash;30%&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/how-coffee-makes-you-live-longer">lower risk of early death</a>&nbsp;compared to non-drinkers (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18559841" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">93<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007%2Fs00125-006-0435-9?LI=true#/page-1" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">94</a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/16968850" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">95<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1047279709001653" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">96</a>).</p>
<p>Just remember that too much caffeine can also lead to anxiety and insomnia, so you may want to curb your intake to the recommended limit of 400 mg per day &mdash; around 4 cups of coffee (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24071782" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">97<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://health.gov/dietaryguidelines/2015-scientific-report/pdfs/scientific-report-of-the-2015-dietary-guidelines-advisory-committee.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">98<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>It&rsquo;s also worth noting that it generally takes six hours for caffeine&rsquo;s effects to subside. Therefore, if you have trouble getting enough high-quality sleep, you may want to shift your intake to earlier in the day.</p>
<h2>13. Develop a good sleeping pattern</h2>
<p><a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/10-reasons-why-good-sleep-is-important">Sleep</a>&nbsp;is crucial for regulating cell function and helping your body heal.</p>
<p>A recent study reports that longevity is likely linked to regular sleeping patterns, such as going to bed and waking up around the same time each day (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4067693/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">99<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Sleep duration also seems to be a factor, with both too little and too much being harmful.</p>
<p>For instance, sleeping less than 5&ndash;7 hours per night is linked to a 12% greater risk of early death, while sleeping more than 8&ndash;9 hours per night could also decrease your lifespan by up to 38% (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2864873/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">100<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1365-2869.2008.00732.x/full" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">101<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>Too little sleep may also promote inflammation and increase your risk of diabetes, heart disease, and&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/10-causes-of-weight-gain">obesity</a>. These are all linked to a shortened lifespan (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17430213" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">102<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19910503" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">103<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21300732" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">104<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>,&nbsp;<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2398753/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">105<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
<p>On the other hand, excessive sleep could be linked to depression, low physical activity, and undiagnosed health conditions, all of which may negatively affect your lifespan (<a class="content-link css-5r4717" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18945686" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">106<span class="css-1471oxf icon-hl-trusted-source-after"><span class="sro">Trusted Source</span></span></a>).</p>
